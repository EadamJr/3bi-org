About 3Bi

Who We Are

The Build Back Better Initiative (3Bi) is an independent policy research think-tank focused on Sudan and East Africa. Unlike conventional think-tanks, we are explicitly grassroots-connected — bridging social movements with evidence-informed policymaking.

Our defining intellectual and organizational orientation is the decolonization of aid and research: amplifying indigenous intellectual capacity, documenting community innovations such as Sudan's Emergency Response Rooms and Resistance Committees, and challenging colonial humanitarian structures while maintaining academic rigor.

3Bi draws on the nafeer tradition — the Sudanese cultural practice of mutual aid — as a conceptual foundation for community-grounded policy solutions.

Our Dual Mandate

Primary: Rigorous, evidence-based policy analysis addressing Sudan and East Africa's challenges.

Secondary: Specialized political solutions for democratic actors, including research and communication analytics.

Vision

A Sudan and East Africa where evidence-based policy-making, inclusive governance, and climate-resilient development enable communities to thrive with dignity and agency.

Mission

3Bi bridges rigorous research, grassroots realities, and policy influence to deliver actionable solutions for Sudan and East Africa's most pressing challenges.

Values

Independence • Decolonization • Community Accountability • Rigor • Transparency • Solidarity

Track Record

Proven in Crisis, Built for Policy

Our milestones showcase our dedication to community-led governance and evidence-based solutions.

2020

COVID-19 Pandemic Response

Coordinated emergency health response across multiple stakeholders during Sudan's pandemic crisis.

  • Developed data collection and analysis systems for health surveillance
  • Built relationships with Federal and State Ministries of Health, WHO, and civil society
2020

Catastrophic Floods Response (Build Back Better)

Led the Build Back Better Project, operationalizing grassroots democratization through community-based governance.

  • Established Primary Healthcare Centers serving displaced populations
  • Created coordination matrices between Resistance Committees and government ministries
2021–Now

Post-Coup Operations & Think-Tank Formalization

Maintained operational capacity during political instability and began think-tank institutionalization.

  • Sustained community engagement and service delivery despite institutional disruptions
  • Developed comprehensive think-tank governance and research frameworks

Thematic Focus

Core Areas of Policy Work

Five primary thematic areas and cross-cutting themes guide 3Bi's research agenda.

🌡️

Climate-Conflict Nexus

Climate change-induced disasters exacerbate ongoing conflicts and displacement. We analyze resource competition, migration patterns, and adaptation strategies in fragile contexts.

🏛️

Governance & Democratization

Post-conflict governance, transitional justice, and democratic institution-building. We document the role of civil society in Sudan's democratic transition.

🤝

Humanitarian-Development Nexus

Integrated approaches beyond emergency response. We analyze pathways from crisis relief to sustainable development and institutional resilience.

🏥

Public Health Systems

Health system resilience, disease surveillance, and community health in conflict-affected settings. We draw on our COVID-19 response experience.

🌱

Grassroots Empowerment

Community-led governance, civil society strengthening, and participatory policy-making. We document innovations like Sudan's Resistance Committees and Emergency Response Rooms.

  • 👥 Youth Leadership
  • 📊 Data & Evidence
